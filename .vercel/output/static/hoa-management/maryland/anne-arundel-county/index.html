The county-specific questions we get.

Ask your own question →
01 How is HOA management different in Anne Arundel County vs. other Maryland counties?

Three things stand out: (1) Waterfront density — Annapolis, Edgewater, and Mayo have a high concentration of waterfront condo associations with master-policy and dock-management complications most Maryland counties don't face. (2) Master-planned scale — Crofton and Odenton have layered HOAs with sub-associations. (3) State capital regulatory access — Annapolis is the seat of state government. Tidewater's Anne Arundel team is sized for all three.

02 Do you serve all of Anne Arundel County?

Yes — from Linthicum and Glen Burnie in the north, to Annapolis and Severna Park in the center, down to Edgewater, Shady Side, and Deale in the south. Our regional manager covers the entire county on a Tuesday/Thursday on-site cadence.

03 How quickly can you start managing our Anne Arundel community?

4-5 weeks from signed proposal for self-managed transitions; 7 weeks when switching from another company. We run the transition on your behalf.

04 What's your fee structure for Anne Arundel HOAs?

Full-service runs $28-$42 per unit per month depending on community size, vendor count, meeting cadence, and complexity. Financial-only tier runs roughly 35-55% of that. We quote line-item with no hidden setup or transition fees.

05 Can we talk to other Anne Arundel boards before signing?

Always. We'll connect you with 3-5 board presidents from comparable Anne Arundel communities — same size, similar stage, similar issues. You call them, no script.

06 What's your Anne Arundel office address?

Our headquarters is in Owings Mills (Baltimore County). Our Anne Arundel regional manager works on-site twice a week and from a satellite address in Severna Park. Main line: (443) 548-0191.
