Why Commercial Boards Switch to Tidewater

Commercial condos aren't residential. Most management companies treat them like they are.

A residential HOA playbook doesn't fit an office condo or a mixed-use CID. We resolve the six issues that drive most commercial association management changes — each backed by an operational standard.

01
Business-Owner Boards

Board members are running businesses, not volunteering on weekends.

Commercial boards have less patience for slow turnaround than residential ones. Same-business-day response standard, with documentation routed directly to the owner's LLC contact — not their personal email.

02
Tenant Turnover

Unit-tenant churn is the operating reality.

Most commercial condos have unit-owners who lease to operating tenants. We track tenant onboarding and ARC review for signage & build-outs as a continuous workflow — not an exception.

03
Mixed-Use Complexity

Residential, office, and retail in one declaration.

Mixed-use commercial improvement districts (CIDs) have to coordinate HVAC, parking, hours-of-operation, deliveries, and trash across uses. We staff a coordination cadence, not a complaint queue.

04
Master Policy &amp; Liability

Commercial coverage is where small mistakes get expensive.

General liability limits, business-interruption coverage, certificate-of-insurance tracking, and master-policy versus unit-owner split need active management, not annual renewal. Every COI is logged in CINC.

05
Fire Code, ADA &amp; Inspections

Inspections are scheduled before letters arrive.

Sprinkler, elevator, alarm, backflow, ADA compliance, building permits — mapped on the annual management plan with auto-triggers 15 days out. No surprise notices of violation.

06
Transition From Residential Mgmt

Switching from a residential company is the most common move.

Most commercial boards we onboard are leaving a residential property manager whose playbook didn't fit. Our transition team has a commercial-condo onboarding playbook with vendor handoff, tenant communication, and CC&R reconciliation.
