Why Coastal Delaware Is Different

Lewes & the Sussex coast, operationally.

Three things owners coming from a Maryland portfolio need to know before listing a Delaware property.

Owner profile

Second-home heavy

A large share of Lewes–Rehoboth rental owners live elsewhere — mostly DC, NoVA, Philadelphia, NJ. We’re built for remote-owner operations: DocuSign agreements, AppFolio owner portal, monthly statements with photos, named manager who walks the property in person.

Same model as our deployed-military Anne Arundel portfolio
Market rhythm

Seasonal demand curve

Coastal Delaware rentals don’t lease on a Mid-Atlantic timeline. Summer-season demand from Memorial Day to Labor Day drives weekly & monthly leases. Year-round long-term leases run a different cadence. [PLACEHOLDER — confirm Tidewater’s scope: long-term, seasonal, or both.]

Different operating model than mainland MD
Regulatory

Landlord-friendlier than Maryland

Delaware has faster eviction timelines, no state-level rent control, no statewide rental license, and no state sales tax. Sussex County communities have local HOA rules — rental caps, minimum-term requirements, architectural review — that are often more restrictive than anything the state imposes. We track them per association.

Math works differently than across the state line
